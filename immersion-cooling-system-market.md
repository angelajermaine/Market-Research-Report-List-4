<p><p>Immersion cooling systems are a type of cooling technology used in data centers to manage heat generated by IT equipment. There are two primary types of immersion cooling systems: single-phase and two-phase. Single-phase immersion cooling systems involve submerging equipment in a dielectric fluid to dissipate heat. Two-phase immersion cooling systems use a boiling liquid to remove heat, providing more efficient cooling. Both types offer benefits such as reduced energy consumption, increased equipment longevity, and improved performance in high-density computing environments.</p></p>
