<p><p>Paralleling switchgear systems can be classified into two main types: linear type and ring type. </p><p>Linear type systems have a straightforward design where generators are connected in a line, allowing for easy expansion by simply adding more units in series. This type is commonly used in smaller installations.</p><p>On the other hand, ring type systems have a more complex design with generators connected in a circular or loop configuration, offering increased reliability and redundancy. This type is typically used in larger and critical applications where uninterrupted power supply is essential.</p></p>
