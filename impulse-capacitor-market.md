<p><p>Impulse capacitors are divided into two main types based on voltage ratings: high voltage impulse capacitors and low voltage impulse capacitors. High voltage impulse capacitors are designed to withstand high levels of voltage spikes and surges, making them suitable for applications where high voltages are present. On the other hand, low voltage impulse capacitors are used in applications where lower voltage levels are involved, providing protection against smaller voltage fluctuations and spikes. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring the stability and reliability of electrical systems.</p></p>
