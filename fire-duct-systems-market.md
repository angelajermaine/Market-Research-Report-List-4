<p><p>Fire duct systems can be categorized into two main types: passive and positive. Passive fire duct systems are designed to contain and restrict the spread of fire and smoke within a building, typically through the use of fire-resistance materials and construction techniques. Positive fire duct systems, on the other hand, actively control the flow of air and smoke within a building during a fire event, helping to maintain safe conditions for occupants and facilitate evacuation. Both types of systems play a critical role in ensuring the safety of buildings and their occupants in the event of a fire.</p></p>
