<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is a technology used to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions in exhaust gases from vehicles or industrial processes. There are three main types of SCR systems: NH3-SCR, Urea-SCR, and Ammonia-SCR. NH3-SCR uses an ammonia-based reducing agent, Urea-SCR uses urea as the reducing agent, and Ammonia-SCR directly injects ammonia into the exhaust stream. Each type of SCR system has its own advantages and disadvantages, depending on the specific application and requirements.</p></p>
