<p><p>Cobalt alloys can be broadly categorized into two main types: Cobalt-Base Wear-Resistant Alloys and Cobalt-Base High-Temperature Alloys. Wear-resistant alloys are specifically designed to withstand abrasive and erosive environments, making them ideal for applications where high wear resistance is needed. On the other hand, high-temperature alloys are formulated to maintain their mechanical properties at elevated temperatures, making them suitable for use in heat-intensive environments such as gas turbines and industrial furnaces. Both types of cobalt alloys offer unique properties and applications in various industries.</p></p>
