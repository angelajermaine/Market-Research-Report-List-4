<p><p>Food texturizing agents are additives used to improve the texture and mouthfeel of food products. Plant-derived agents, such as starches and gums, are commonly used for their natural properties. Microbial-derived agents, like enzymes, are produced by microorganisms and help modify textures. Seaweed-derived agents, such as carrageenan, are popular for their gelling properties. Animal-derived agents, like gelatin, provide unique textures and structures. Synthetic agents are artificially created to mimic natural textures. Each type offers different benefits and applications in the food industry.</p></p>
